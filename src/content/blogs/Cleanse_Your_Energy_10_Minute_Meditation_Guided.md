## How to Prepare for Your Energy Cleanse

### Create a Quiet Sacred Space

You don't need a dedicated temple. Stack two pillows in the corner of your bedroom, or drag a chair to a window. The key is consistency. Your nervous system learns through repetition—returning to the same spot signals safety to your brain. Remove one visual distraction (that pile of laundry you've been meaning to fold) and silence phone notifications. This isn't about perfection; it's about intention.

### Find a Comfortable Seated Position

Forget the pretzel-legged stereotype. Sit in a chair with feet flat, or cross-legged on a cushion—whatever lets your spine feel tall without strain. Roll your shoulders back once, like you're opening a book, and let your palms rest upward on your thighs. This simple hand position, confirmed by somatic psychology research, signals receptivity to your nervous system, making release easier.

### Set a Clear Intention

Before you begin, complete this sentence: "Right now, I release..." Maybe it's "the tension from my deadline" or "the anger from that awkward text exchange." Verbalizing creates a cognitive container. Studies in behavioral psychology show that naming an emotion reduces its intensity by up to 50%.

## Step-by-Step 10-Minute Guided Energy Cleanse

### Minutes 0-2: Grounding and Centering

Sit and close your eyes lovingly—not forced, but like you're greeting an old friend. Feel the surface beneath you. Imagine roots growing from your sit bones, traveling through floors, soil, bedrock, deep into the earth's stable core. This isn't metaphorical fluff; it's proprioceptive feedback that tells your brain you're secure enough to relax.

Take three deep breaths: inhale through your nose for 4 counts, hold for 1, then exhale forcefully through your mouth for 6. This extended exhale stimulates the vagus nerve, your body's built-in calm-down switch. Research shows this breathing pattern drops cortisol levels within two minutes.

### Minutes 2-5: Breathwork and Body Scan

Return to natural breathing. Let your stomach expand on inhale, contract on exhale. Now scan your body like a slow-motion camera, starting at the crown of your head. Notice any areas that feel dense, buzzing, or stuck—maybe it's a knot between your shoulders, a buzzing in your chest, or a hollow feeling in your gut.

**Here's the science**: Neuroscientist Dr. Candace Pert discovered that neuropeptides—molecules of emotion—cluster in specific body areas. Unprocessed feelings literally get stored in tissue. Your scan is locating these clusters. When you find tension, breathe directly into it. Imagine your inhale as a warm flashlight, illuminating the darkness.

One teacher explains: "I picture each breath like ocean waves eroding sharp rocks—gradually smoothing what felt jagged."

### Minutes 5-8: Visualization and Release

See an orb of champagne-colored light hovering above your head. This soft, golden light—like sunlight through amber—begins to pour down in a continuous shower. It washes over your scalp, dissolving worry. Down your face, melting away the mask you wear for others. Through your neck and shoulders, carrying the weight of responsibilities that aren't yours to bear.

The light moves through your arms—watch it dissolve the tension from endless typing, the clenched fists from held-back words. Feel it pour through your torso, clearing stuck frustration from your rib cage, anxiety from your stomach. It flows down your legs, through knees that feel ready to give out, and out through your feet into the earth.

The key is allowing, not forcing. Your only job is to breathe and witness. Studies in psychoneuroimmunology confirm that this type of passive visualization reduces inflammatory cytokines—the molecules that cause physical stress symptoms.

**What if your mind wanders?** Perfect. Each return to the visualization strengthens neural pathways like reps at a gym. The goal isn't emptiness—it's presence.

### Minutes 8-10: Sealing Positive Energy

Once your body feels clear and translucent—like glass filled with light—imagine the ring of golden light expanding wider. Now it's washing over your energetic body, your aura, that space extending about a foot around you (or three feet, or whatever feels right). Trust what you sense.

This light now forms a gentle boundary, not a wall. It's permeable—good things still get in, but energy drains bounce off. Picture it like a cell membrane: intelligent, selective, protective.

Research from the HeartMath Institute shows that maintaining a coherent energetic field improves decision-making and emotional regulation for up to 6 hours post-meditation.

Place a hand on your heart. Feel gratitude for this time you've given yourself. Gratitude amplifies the practice, increasing dopamine and serotonin naturally.
