## Why Grounding Works: The Science of Soft Presence

It is not just a time of silence, but a true optimization of the nervous system. The fundamental teaching of this practice is that of non-judgment and surrender to control and to be a gentle witness to your own experience.

When you start focusing your mind on what is happening outside and change to the inner experiences of your breath and body, this triggers the parasympathetic nervous system (PNS) or the natural rest and digest system of your body. This is an effective counter of the stress signal as relayed by the fight or flight (sympathetic) response.

This particular purpose of this brief session focus on **anchoring and non-doing**:

*   **Deep Anchoring:** This is going to happen because you will be led to experience the sensation of the earth pulling you down by the effect of gravitational force and creating an immediate sense of stability.
*   **Reduced Mental Chatter:** You are trained to change from not being an "active participant" in your mental processes, but rather an "Observer." This is the main art of mindfulness that makes one not reactive.
*   **Full Body Relaxation:** The command to just allow any muscles that you do not need to hold on to at the moment makes the process of physical release of unconscious tension easier.
